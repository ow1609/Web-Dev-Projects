Onuora's Favourite Gooey Vegan Brownie Recipe

Credit:

This recipe is one I have taken from The Big Man's World by Arman Liew. I have made several batches of these brownies for friends and family who absolutely love them. The brownies are truly delicious and so gooey inside. Perfect for special dietry requirements, I have even made gluten-free versions and they are absolutely top knotch!

Ingredients (12 servings):

  • 30g ground flaxseed
  • 90ml brewed coffee can use for water
  • 225g vegan chocolate chips divided in half
  • 160g brown sugar
  • 90g vegan butter or margarine
  • 5ml vanilla extract
  • 95 g plain flour can use gluten-free all purpose flour
  • 5ml salt
  • 5ml baking powder
  • 30g cocoa powder

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C/350F. Line a square pan with parchment paper and set aside.
  2. Prepare your flax "egg" by combining your ground flaxseed with your brewed (chilled) coffee. Let it sit for 10 minutes, to form a gel.
  3. In a heat-proof bowl placed over a pan of boiling water, melt half of the chocolate chips (112g or 1 cup). Set aside.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, add your vegan butter and sugar, and whisk well.
  5. Add in your prepared flax eggs, melted chocolate, vanilla extract, and mix well, until glossy.
  6. Sift through your flour, baking powder, salt, and cocoa powder and mix until just combined. Do not overmix.
  7. Fold through the remaining chocolate chips.
  8. Transfer your brownie batter into the lined pan.
  9. Bake your brownies for 27-30 minutes.
  10. Remove brownies from the oven and let cool completely, before slicing into 12 pieces.

STORAGE:

Refrigerator:

Store leftovers in the refrigerator, to ensure they remain fresh. They are super gooey and fudgy, so the chilled temperature will ensure they don't spoil. Consume within a few days if you manage not to gobble them up right awat in one sitting!

Freezer:

Wrap them in parchment paper individually, and place them in a ziplock bag and store them in the freezer. They will keep well frozen for up to 6 months.
